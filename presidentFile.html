Consitutional Monarchy

  • New Zealand is a parliamentary democracy under a constitutional monarchy,
    known for its stability,transparency, and fair representation.
  • Head of State: The Monarch (Ceremonial Role) King Charles III is New Zealand’s
    official head of state but does not govern. His duties are carried out by the Governor-General
    (currently Dame Cindy Kiro), who signs laws, appoints ministers, and represents the country.
  • 120 Members of Parliament (MPs) are elected every 3 years using MMP
    (Mixed-Member Proportional) voting: Party Vote determines total seats (e.g., 40% vote = ~48 seats).
    Electorate Vote chooses local MPs. Ensures diverse representation (e.g., Māori seats, minor parties).
  • No Written Constitution (But Key Laws & Conventions) Rules come from: Treaty of Waitangi (1840)
    (Māori-Crown partnership). Constitution Act 1986 (defines government roles). Electoral Act 1993 (establishes MMP voting).
  • Independent Courts & Local Government Courts operate free from political influence.
    Regional councils manage local issues (e.g., transport, environment).
  • Did You Know? New Zealand was the first country to grant women the vote (1893)!
